**Executive Summary**



During the six-month analysis period, the business generated approximately £4.0 million in revenue from 2,685 unique customers, with an average revenue of £1,490 per customer. Sales peaked in December 2009, reflecting strong seasonal demand, before declining in the following months and partially recovering in March 2010.



The United Kingdom was by far the largest market, contributing the vast majority of total revenue, while international markets generated comparatively modest sales. Revenue was also concentrated among a relatively small proportion of products: 919 products (24.2% of the 3,800 products) accounted for 80% of total revenue, indicating that a focused product portfolio drives most business performance.



Customer segmentation showed a healthy base of Champions and Loyal Customers, but also identified At Risk customers who may require targeted retention efforts. Overall, the analysis highlights opportunities to improve revenue by strengthening customer retention, optimizing the product portfolio, and expanding sales in international markets.

**Key Business Takeaways / Actions**



1\. Protect the UK Market



The United Kingdom is the primary revenue source and should remain the focus of customer retention and service excellence.



2\. Expand International Sales



Markets such as EIRE, the Netherlands, and Germany present opportunities to diversify revenue and reduce dependence on a single country.



3\. Prioritize High-Performing Products



Maintain stock availability and promotional focus for the 919 products responsible for 80% of total revenue while reviewing lower-performing products for optimization.



4\. Invest in Customer Retention



Champions and Loyal Customers represent valuable long-term revenue. Retention strategies are typically more cost-effective than acquiring new customers.



5\. Re-engage At Risk Customers



Targeted campaigns and personalized incentives can help recover customers before they become inactive.



6\. Prepare for Seasonal Demand



Plan inventory, staffing, and marketing activities around the strong December sales period while implementing promotions during slower months to smooth revenue fluctuations.



7\. Use Customer Insights to Drive Growth



Continue leveraging RFM segmentation and high-value customer analysis to personalize marketing, improve customer experience, and maximize customer lifetime value.







**Project Limitations**



Time Period: This analysis covers the available six-month period (December 2009 to May 2010). Trends beyond this timeframe are outside the scope of the dataset.

Revenue-Based Analysis: The dataset does not include product cost or profit information. Therefore, the analysis focuses on revenue rather than profitability.

Customer Information: Customer IDs are anonymized identifiers. Customer demographics and other personal details are not available, limiting customer analysis to purchasing behavior.

Product Information: The dataset contains product descriptions but does not include product categories, supplier information, or inventory levels, limiting deeper product performance analysis.

Historical Dataset: Findings are based on the historical Online Retail II dataset and should be interpreted as a case study rather than current business performance.
